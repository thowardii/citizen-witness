# Guardian Ad Litem Device — Core Concept

A recording device whose only job is to preserve the record for the person being watched. It acts as a legal guardian for the footage.

---

## The Analogy

A child cannot represent themselves in court. The state appoints a guardian ad litem — an independent advocate whose sole function is to investigate and tell the court what is in the child's best interest.

Apply this to surveillance. Every camera pointed at you — body cams, dash cams, ALPRs, security footage — captures evidence that could exonerate or incriminate you. But you don't have access to most of it. You're the child in that transaction.

The device becomes the guardian. Its only job: preserve the record for your defense.

---

## Four Properties

### 1. Tamper-Proof

The device cannot be muted, paused, or stopped by anyone other than the citizen who activated it. The recording is encrypted and chained — each segment hashes the previous segment. Gaps are detectable. Alterations are provable.

### 2. Chain-of-Custody Verified

Every transfer of the recording — from device to cloud, from cloud to lawyer, from lawyer to court — is logged with timestamps and hashes. The file either exists with a verified chain, or it doesn't. No "we lost it." No "the footage corrupted." The chain proves exactly when and where any break occurred.

### 3. Absence as Evidence

Because the device is known to be tamper-proof and chain-of-custody verified, its absence in any legal proceeding is itself a fact. If the recording was active during an encounter and it disappears while in government custody, that is spoliation. The absence becomes the argument.

### 4. Guardian Ad Litem Posture

The device does not resist. It does not escalate. It does not argue with the officer. It simply preserves — and if it's taken, disabled, or destroyed, that act is recorded in the chain log up to the point of interruption. The device's final act before silence is to timestamp exactly when it was silenced.

---

## Legal Hooks

- **Spoliation of evidence:** Destroying, altering, or failing to preserve evidence for another's use in reasonably foreseeable litigation can justify sanctions or adverse inferences against the responsible party.
- **Adverse inference:** When a party destroys or fails to produce evidence, the jury may infer that the evidence would have been unfavorable to that party.
- **"No tape, no testimony":** Some body camera statutes and model policies allow juries to draw adverse inferences when required recordings are missing or tampered with.

---

## Why This Exists

Because police body cameras exist to protect departments from liability. A citizen-side device with the same properties — tamper-proof, chain-of-custody verified, guardian posture — protects the citizen from the encounter. The asymmetry is the point.

The device doesn't prevent misconduct. It makes misconduct provable. And if the device disappears while in their custody, the absence is the weapon.
