## Query 2: Probable Cause as the Gate

**Nieves v. Bartlett (2019):** Probable cause generally defeats retaliatory arrest claims. Narrow exception: "objective evidence that otherwise similarly situated individuals not engaged in protected speech had not been arrested."

**Lower courts narrowed Nieves:** Demanded specific "comparator" — a nearly identical person who committed the exact same infraction but wasn't arrested.

**Gonzalez v. Trevino (2024) reopened it:** SCOTUS ruled comparator requirement "goes too far." Plaintiffs can use statistical surveys, unusual enforcement patterns, or anomalous investigation procedures instead.

**Case mapping:**
- **Bailey:** No probable cause at all → PC barrier didn't apply → won damages
- **Novak:** Arguable probable cause → PC barrier blocked claim → no remedy despite constitutional violation
- **Gonzalez:** Technical probable cause → used Nieves exception (statistical survey) → SCOTUS revived case

## Query 3: Recurring Evidence Categories

Five patterns across all three cases:

1. **Unusual enforcement / disproportionate escalation** — SWAT for a joke, felony for a parody, special detective for a document mix-up
2. **Pretextual charges that collapse** — terrorism, disrupting public services, tampering — all dropped, dismissed, or acquitted
3. **Timing and deliberation gaps** — not split-second decisions. Gonzalez: 2 days. Novak: weeks. Officers had time to investigate.
4. **Officer statements and motive indicators** — warrant affidavit language, mayor's personal involvement, documented complaints
5. **Recordings and spoliation** — surveillance video preserved = helped Gonzalez. Seized devices = spoliation risk. Missing footage becomes evidence.

## Citizen Documentation Strategy

From the pattern library, what a citizen should capture:

- **Preserve the original speech** — screenshot it, save it, timestamp it. The speech IS the evidence.
- **Tamper-proof recording** — device that can't be muted. If it goes silent in police custody, the gap IS the case.
- **Structured timeline** — second-by-second: what was said, who spoke, what action taken.
- **Flag procedural anomalies** — mayor involved in a minor beef? SWAT for a Facebook post? Document the disproportionality.
