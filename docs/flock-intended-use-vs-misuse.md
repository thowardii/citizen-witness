# Flock Safety — Intended Use vs. Documented Misuse

**Research date:** June 22, 2026
**Sources:** EFF 2025 Year in Review, IJ report on ALPR stalking, Flock Safety Law Enforcement Access Policy, ACLU, UW Center for Human Rights, MRSC analysis of SB 6002

---

## Flock's Stated Intended Use

Per Flock's own policy and marketing:
- Crime prevention and investigation
- Locating stolen vehicles via hotlist alerts
- Generating investigative leads
- Solving violent crimes
- "Enhancing community safety"

Official safeguards claimed:
- Searches are tied to individual users with audit logs
- Supervisor review and agency policy enforcement
- Data "never shared automatically" between agencies
- Purpose limitation: only for criminal investigations

---

## Documented Misuse (EFF, ACLU, IJ, UWCHR)

### 1. Surveillance of Protesters and Activists
- 12+ million searches logged by 3,900+ agencies (Dec 2024–Oct 2025)
- Hundreds of searches targeting political demonstrations: 50501 protests, Hands Off, No Kings
- 19 agencies conducted dozens of searches specifically for No Kings protests
- Delaware State Police queried Flock 9 times to target Direct Action Everywhere activists
- Some searches explicitly referenced protest activity; others used vague terminology to obscure surveillance of protected speech

### 2. Racial and Ethnic Profiling
- 80+ law enforcement agencies used language perpetuating stereotypes against Romani people
- Searches included terms like "roma traveler," "possible g*psy," and racial slurs — often without mentioning any suspected crime
- Grand Prairie PD (Texas) searched for the slur 6 times while using Flock's "Convoy" feature — targeting an entire traveling community
- 40% of Romani Americans report being subjected to racial profiling by police (Harvard, 2020)

### 3. Weaponized Against Reproductive Healthcare
- Texas deputies queried Flock data for what was characterized as a "missing person" case but was actually an abortion investigation
- A Johnson County official ran two searches with the note "had an abortion, search for female"
- One search probed 6,809 networks, accessing **83,345 cameras** across nearly the entire country
- This case revealed: a single query can access 83,000+ cameras spanning the entire nation with minimal oversight

### 4. Stalking and Personal Misuse
- IJ documented at least 14 cases of police using ALPR networks to stalk romantic interests
- Officers accessed the system for personal reasons, not law enforcement purposes

### 5. Warrantless Mass Surveillance
- San Jose PD and other California agencies searched the database **3,965,519 times** in a single year (June 2024–June 2025)
- EFF + ACLU of Northern California filed lawsuit challenging warrantless searches of millions of ALPR records
- CBP accessed Illinois data in violation of state privacy laws — exposed by EFF, triggered Illinois Secretary of State audit

### 6. ALPRs Becoming General Surveillance Cameras
- Flock announced police departments will be able to use plate readers as general surveillance cameras — far beyond license plate reading
- Adding always-listening voice detection technology

## Regulatory Response

- U.S. Reps. Krishnamoorthi and Garcia launched formal congressional investigation into Flock
- Illinois Secretary of State Giannoulias launched audit after EFF exposed CBP access violating state law
- Washington SB 6002: restricts ALPR data sharing between agencies, limits retention
- Oregon SB 1516: permitted/prohibited uses, audit requirements
- Toledo hearing June 23, 2026: Ordinance O-286-26 for one-year continuation

## The Core Problem (EFF)

"Flock's business model depends on building a nationwide, interconnected surveillance network that creates risks no software update can eliminate. Abuses stem from the architecture itself, not just how individual agencies use the technology."

New lane confirmed: **Surveillance Infrastructure as Retaliation Enabler**

- This is continuous, passive, warrantless surveillance at national scale
- A single query accesses 83,000+ cameras
- No individual officer decision required — the infrastructure IS the violation

- Protester tracking, activist targeting, reproductive surveillance, and racial profiling are not bugs — they are predictable outcomes of the architecture
