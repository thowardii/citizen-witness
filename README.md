# Citizen Witness

A system that turns a recording into a rights-aware witness artifact.

**What it is:** A post-encounter evidence brief generator. Feed it a recording of a police encounter. It produces a structured, lawyer-ready document analyzing the interaction for constitutional violations — starting with First Amendment retaliation.

**What it produced:** [Novak v. City of Parma — Post-Hoc Brief](docs/novak-brief-v1.md). Anthony Novak was arrested in Ohio for parodying his police department on Facebook. The brief analyzes the encounter across seven sections and cross-references the identical legal pattern in *Bailey v. Iles* (Louisiana, 2020).

**How it works:**
1. Recording → transcription
2. Transcription → structured timeline
3. Timeline → rights checklist + probable cause analysis
4. Analysis → pattern match against known retaliation cases
5. Pattern match → seven-section brief a lawyer can file

**v0.1 scope:** First Amendment retaliation only. Narrow on purpose.

**Legal spine:**
- Parody and satire are protected speech
- Arrest in retaliation for protected speech violates the First Amendment
- A device designed to preserve the record makes its absence evidence of spoliation
- The same pattern recurs: speech → police retaliation → arrest → prosecution dropped or acquitted → §1983 suit → qualified immunity fight

**Why:** Because citizens who record their encounters with law enforcement should walk away with more than a video file. They should walk away with a document their lawyer can use.
